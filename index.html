Places to Hangout in Boston

Hello everyone. Welcome to my blog webpage!

Are you new to Boston? Trying to find some nice hangout places?
If so, I got you covered! I will include three of my favorite places to hangout in Boston and how to get there.
Let's get into it!

Boston Common

Did you know Boston Boston Common is the oldest park in the U.S. Whether it is the summer or winter
there is always something to do. You can find fairs/festivals in the common park. You can even watch free theater
someday if you're lucky. You can ride on a swan boat in Boston Common pond. I always go there with my friends
because it is a nice and quiet place and you can smell the freshness in the air. During winter, you can skate in the
frog pond and explore different clothing stores right next to it.

Public Commuting options:

If you are in Boston already, you can take the red line or the green line based on your current
location and get off at Park Street station.

Charles River Esplanade

The bank of Charles River which is 17 miles stretch of land is one of the most beautiful places in the
Boston. Whether you want to go for a walk or bike in the evening this is the place
you want to go. You can also kayak in summer. They also added a new playground for the
kids. This place is the perfect sunset spot. So, next time you are in Boston and want to watch
the sunset, now you know where to go!

Public Commuting options:

You can take the Red line train and get off at the Charles MGH Station.

Boston Harbor

You like the ocean and want to feel the fresh breeze and hear the sounds of water,
Boston Harbor is the place to go. They have 40 miles of pedestrian- and bike-friendly public
access at the water edge. They also have boat rides by which you can get to some of the
beautiful islands. You can also get tickets for whale watching boats. Overall, it is a great place
to spend time with family and friends.

Public Commuting options:

From the Downtown crossing, you can take the blue line to Orient Heights station. From there,
you can take the 712 MBTA bus and get off at Deer Island station.

Public Commuting options and fares:

Places Commute fares
Boston Common Red line or Green line train $2
Charles River Esplanade Red line train $2
Boston Harbor Blue line train + 712 MBTA Bus $12
